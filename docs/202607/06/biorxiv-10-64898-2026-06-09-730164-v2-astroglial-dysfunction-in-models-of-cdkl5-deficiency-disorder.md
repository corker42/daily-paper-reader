## 摘要
CDKL5缺乏症（CDD）是一种罕见的发育性癫痫性脑病，通常由编码X连锁丝氨酸-苏氨酸激酶CDKL5的基因功能丧失变异引起。CDKL5在发育期的大脑中有高表达，其关键的神经元功能包括细胞骨架组织和突触稳定性。然而，目前关于星形胶质细胞在CDD中的功能知之甚少。鉴于这些细胞在突触发育和稳态中的重要性，以及其他癫痫疾病中的功能障碍，我们假设星形胶质细胞可能参与CDD病理过程。携带CDKL5功能丧失突变（以及同基因对照）的诱导多能干细胞来自CDD患者成纤维细胞，并分化为星形胶质细胞（iAstros）。对iAstros的分析显示，CDKL5突变型iAstros在与水运输和免疫功能相关的转录组、蛋白质组和功能上存在失调，包括对TNF刺激的反应减弱。此外，iAstros显示出分支增加和已知CDKL5靶点末端结合蛋白2（EB2）的磷酸化减少——这表明细胞骨架调节受损，方式类似于CDKL5缺失的神经元。最后，我们报告了新型CDD体外模型的生成。通过使用编码新型CDKL5 shRNA的AAV转导成人和胎儿人器官型脑切片，敲低了CDKL5。转导CDKL5 shRNA的脑切片显示出自发性网络活动增加，证明了该模型的功能性。重要的是，对这些模型的检测揭示了关键星形胶质细胞蛋白的失调，与人类胶质干细胞模型一致。因此，本研究描述了新型人类CDD模型及其相关的星形胶质细胞功能障碍的生成——为新的发现和治疗干预铺平了道路。

## Abstract
CDKL5 Deficiency Disorder (CDD) is a rare developmental epileptic encephalopathy typically caused by loss of function variants in the gene encoding the X-linked serine-threonine kinase CDKL5. CDKL5 is highly expressed in the brain during development, and key neuronal functions of the kinase include cytoskeletal organisation and synaptic stability. However, at present, little is known about the function of astroglia in CDD. Given the importance of these cells in synaptic development and homeostasis, as well as dysfunction in other epileptic diseases, it was hypothesised that astrocytes may contribute to CDD pathology. Induced pluripotent stem cells harbouring a CDKL5 loss-of-function mutation (and isogenic controls) were derived from CDD patient fibroblasts and differentiated into astrocytes (iAstros). Analysis of iAstros revealed transcriptomic, proteomic and functional dysregulation in CDKL5-mutant iAstros relating to water transport and immunological function, including a diminished response to TNF stimulation. Moreover, iAstros showed increased branching and reduced phosphorylation of the known CDKL5 target end-binding protein 2 (EB2) - indicative of disrupted cytoskeletal regulation in a manner similar to CDKL5-null neurons. Finally, we report the generation of novel in vitro models of CDD. CDKL5 was knocked down in adult and foetal human organotypic brain slices through transduction with an AAV encoding a novel CDKL5 shRNA. Slices transduced with the CDKL5 shRNA displayed increased spontaneous network activity, demonstrating the functionality of this model. Importantly, interrogation of these models revealed dysregulation of key astrocytic proteins congruous with the human glial stem cell model. Consequently, this study describes the generation of novel human models of CDD and their associated astrocytic dysfunction - paving the way for novel discovery and therapeutic intervention.
